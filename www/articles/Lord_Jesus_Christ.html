Who is the Lord Jesus Christ?

This is the simplest question to true Christians, because you would be asking them about their king, their Lord, their elder brother, their salvation and their only door to the Father. They don’t know about Him, they know Him. He is the one who they use His name to talk to the Father.

This is how I know Him:

He is the Son of God, the one who is absolutely dependent on God, the one who recognizes that God is high, and that God is even higher than Him. Lord Jesus is the only one whom I can call a representation of God our Father, He is the one who loves the laws of the Almighty God. The doer of the Will of God. He is the good Shepherd who laid down his life for His sheep. The one who does not do things to get glory of men, He is one who does things to Glorify the Father. The one who lived perfectly on earth even though He was tempted. The one who is higher than David, the one who is higher than Solomon, and He is the one who existed even before Abraham. He is the great high priest. He is the expression of the wonderful Grace of God. He is the Truth, the Way and the Life, and if any man enters through Him, he will be saved. He is the great key which can open any lock, He is the heavy hammer which smashes all types of slave chains, and He is the sufficient payment for all types of sins. I say that as a witness not just a reader.

And a voice from heaven said, “ This is My one dear Son; in Him I take great delight.”

Matthew 3:17

He trusts in God – let God, if He wants to, deliver Him now because He said, ‘I am God’s Son’!”

Matthew 27:43

The beginning of the gospel of Jesus Christ, the Son of God.

Mark 1:1

You have heard Me tell you, ‘I am going away and I am coming to you.’ If you loved Me, you would have rejoiced that I am going to the Father, because the Father is greater than I.

John 14:28 (Lord Jesus Christ)

Then Jesus replied, “I assure you: The Son is not able to do anything on His own, but only what He sees the Father doing. For whatever the Father does, the Son also does these things in the same way.

John 5:19 (Lord Jesus Christ)

I have glorified You on the earth by completing the work You gave Me to do.

John 17:4 (Lord Jesus Christ)

Lord Jesus Christ is one who lived perfectly upon the earth but died like a sinner, because He was sent by the Father so that He may carry our sins and be punished for them, and by doing that we were given an opportunity to holiness just as God is Holy, and finally be saved from Hell. The Lord Jesus Christ being crucified on the cross for sinners like me and you, reveals to us two things about Him,one, He was prepared to do any work that God gave Him even if it took His life. secondly, it tells us that the Lord Jesus Christ is the true representation of true love that surpasses logic, and the love He represents is the love which is super, mega, radiant, grandeur and incomparable, and that love is the Love of God. He is the one who was resurrected by the Father.

For God so loved the world, that He gave His only-begotten Son, that whoever believeth in Him, should not perish, but have everlasting life.

John 3:16 (Lord Jesus Christ)

Last but not least, He is the one who knows the Father, and lets us know that it is not possible to know God if He does not reveal him to you. And if you know God then you know the Lord Jesus Christ, because the Lord Jesus Christ represents the Father. And He is the author and finisher of faith, because without him I personally know that my faith would be dead and without works, but with Him it is alive and it produces good fruit.

All things have been handed over to me by my Father. No one knows the Son except the Father, and no one knows the Father except the Son and anyone to whom the Son decides to reveal him.

Matthew 11:27 (Lord Jesus Christ)

“I am the true vine and my Father is the gardener. John 15:2 He takes away every branch that does not bear fruit in me. He prunes every branch that bears fruit so that it will bear more fruit. You are clean already because of the word that I have spoken to you. Remain in me, and I will remain in you. Just as the branch cannot bear fruit by itself, unless it remains in the vine, so neither can you unless you remain in me.
